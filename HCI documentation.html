VRINGO

Barascu Bianca, Marin Andrei-Vasile, Sorohan Dumitrita

Abstract

VRINGO is an innovative mixed reality (MR) enhanced shopping assistant designed to help busy parents efficiently manage grocery shopping with minimal time investment. This project combines mobile application technology with mixed reality capabilities to create a seamless shopping experience. The system connects to grocery delivery services like Bringo while providing real-time information about store inventory and shopping assistants. The MR component allows users to visualize products in 3D/AR along with relevant nutritional information and alternatives. This documentation outlines the design methodology, interaction patterns, user personas, and interface prototypes that form the VRINGO system.

Keywords: Mixed Reality, Human-Computer Interaction, Grocery Shopping, Mobile Application, User Experience Design, Augmented Reality

Introduction

Modern families, particularly parents with young children, often struggle to balance professional responsibilities with household management tasks like grocery shopping. Traditional grocery shopping methods require significant time investment, while existing delivery apps lack personalization and real-time product verification capabilities.

VRINGO addresses these challenges by creating a dual-platform solution:

  • A mobile application that connects to shopping services like Bringo
  • A mixed reality (MR) headset application that enhances the shopping experience with 3D visualization and detailed product information

The system enables busy parents to efficiently manage household provisions while maintaining control over product selection quality, without the time commitment of physical shopping. The MR component adds a layer of information and interactivity that is not possible with traditional shopping applications.

This documentation outlines the research methodology, design principles, user personas, interaction patterns, and technical specifications of the VRINGO system.

Research Methodology

The VRINGO project was informed by multiple research methodologies to ensure a user-centered design approach:

Literature Review

A comprehensive literature review was conducted to understand current trends in online grocery shopping behavior and mixed reality shopping applications. Key resources included Mercatus research on shopping behavior and academic papers on mixed reality in e-commerce contexts.

Competitor Analysis

An in-depth analysis of existing grocery delivery platforms in the Romanian market was performed, focusing on four main competitors:

Criteria Bringo Glovo Tazz Bolt
Geographic Coverage Large and medium cities, limited coverage Leader, present in 75+ Romanian cities Major cities, expanding Major urban centers, more limited than Glovo
Merchant Variety Predominantly Carrefour supermarkets, pharmacies, pet shops Highly diversified: restaurants, supermarkets, florists, gifts Restaurants, supermarkets, integration with eMAG Mostly restaurants and fast food, more limited range
Delivery Time ~120 minutes for supermarket orders 30-60 minutes depending on distance 30-60 minutes, variable 30-45 minutes, optimized for delivery speed
Order Customization Real-time modification through communication with delivery person Limited customization options Allows delivery instructions and orders Standard customization options
Ecosystem Integration Part of the Carrefour ecosystem Independent application with own store (Glovo Express) Integration with eMAG and Genius system Part of the Bolt ecosystem (transport, delivery)

The analysis identified key strengths and weaknesses in current offerings:

  • Strengths to incorporate: Real-time communication with shoppers (Bringo), wide merchant variety (Glovo), ecosystem integration (Tazz, Bolt)
  • Gaps to address: Limited product visualization, lack of nutritional information standardization, inconsistent stock updates, and limited personalization

User Personas

Three detailed personas were developed based on research to represent the primary user types for the VRINGO system:

Elena - The Busy Working Mother

Demographics:

  • Age: 43 (Born: 08-03-1980)
  • Gender: Female
  • Location: Iasi, Codrescu Campus
  • Occupation: Software Developer at Palas Campus
  • Education: Master's in Information Systems, Iasi
  • Technology Level: Advanced

Psychographics:

Elena is a caring mother focused on her family's wellbeing. She works 8-9 hours daily, plus commute time. Due to her busy schedule, she relies on her mother-in-law to help with childcare when she and her husband are at work.

Goals:

Prioritizing her family, Elena wants to ensure everyone has everything they need, including fresh food and other necessities, while balancing her professional responsibilities.

Frustrations:

Her biggest frustration with current delivery services is that couriers don't always verify product quality, which is particularly important for fresh groceries.

Scenario:

After work, Elena can't go shopping physically because she knows her mother-in-law is exhausted from looking after her energetic child. She needs an intelligent application that can show her the condition of products before purchase, including expiration dates and freshness indicators.

Psychographics:

Liviu deeply loves his family but, like Elena, occasionally needs to work overtime. During his free time, he takes the family for walks and handles necessary household shopping.

Liviu's primary goal is to arrive home on time to spend quality time with his family and relieve his mother from childcare duties.

Frustrations:

Limited time prevents him from fully executing family plans, with shopping taking up valuable time he could spend with his family.

Scenario:

Liviu understands time limitations and agrees with Elena that they're both very busy with work. He needs a solution that minimizes time spent on logistics like shopping while maintaining household quality standards.

Storyboards and User Flows

Storyboards were created to visualize key user interactions with both the mobile and MR components of VRINGO:

Mobile Application User Flow

The mobile application flow follows these key steps:

  1. User authentication and profile selection
  2. Store/merchant selection from available options
  3. Category browsing and product search
  4. Product selection with real-time inventory verification
  5. Cart review with substitution options
  6. Shopper assignment and real-time communication
  7. Delivery scheduling and tracking
  8. Order completion and feedback

MR Headset Application User Flow

The MR headset application flow comprises:

  1. MR environment initialization and calibration
  2. Synchronization with mobile app profile and current shopping session
  3. Virtual store navigation through gesture controls
  4. Product visualization in 3D with nutritional information overlay
  5. Product comparison mode for alternatives
  6. Add-to-cart functionality with haptic feedback
  7. Virtual shopping list management
  8. Handoff to mobile app for checkout and delivery

Icon Style

The icon system uses a consistent flat design with rounded edges, maintaining a 2px stroke width and 24x24px base size. Icons are designed to be legible in both 2D mobile interface and 3D MR environment.

UI Component Style

UI components follow these consistent design principles:

  • Button style: Rounded corners (8px radius), clear hover states, consistent padding
  • Card style: Subtle shadows (2px blur, 30% opacity), rounded corners (12px)
  • Input fields: Clearly defined with 1px borders, 40px height, and visual feedback states
  • Spacing system: Based on 8px increments for consistent rhythm across the interface

Interaction Design Patterns

Mobile Application Interaction Patterns

The mobile application employs several established interaction patterns optimized for on-the-go use:

Card-Based Content Organization

Products and categories are presented in a card-based layout that provides sufficient information while maintaining visual clarity.

Design Rationale: Card layouts allow for scannable content with clear visual hierarchy, supporting users who need to make quick decisions while potentially distracted.

Progressive Disclosure

Information is revealed progressively as users navigate deeper into the product selection process.

Design Rationale: This prevents cognitive overload for busy parents by showing only what's necessary at each decision point, while making detailed information available on demand.

Pull-to-Refresh & Infinite Scrolling

Content is refreshed with a pull gesture, and additional products load automatically as users scroll.

Design Rationale: These familiar patterns reduce friction in browsing large catalogs of products while providing clear feedback about content updates.

MR Headset Interaction Patterns

The MR application introduces spatial interaction patterns optimized for the mixed reality environment:

Spatial Anchoring

UI elements and 3D product models are anchored in physical space, allowing users to interact with them naturally.

Design Rationale: This leverages spatial memory and reduces cognitive load by mimicking real-world shopping behavior.

Gaze and Gesture Combination

Interaction combines gaze targeting with hand gestures for selection and manipulation.

Design Rationale: This two-step approach prevents accidental interactions while providing natural control over 3D objects.
